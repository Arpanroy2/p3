4.6 BYA: molecular cloud collapses → supernova trigger. Condensation sequence: Refractory metals (1300 K) → silicates/iron (500–1300 K) → water ice (150–500 K) → methane ices (<150 K).

Accretion stages

  • Stage 1: Dust → planetesimals (10k–100k years)
  • Stage 2: Runaway growth (1–10 Myr), Moon‑Mars sized embryos
  • Stage 3: Late‑stage giant impacts (30–100 Myr) – Moon‑forming impact 4.52 BYA

Theia impact: Mars‑sized, 45° off‑center, energy 100 million trillion bombs, created Moon in ~100 years. Evidence: identical mantle composition, small iron core, fast early rotation.

Differentiation: iron sank (<30 Myr), silicates rose → core, mantle, primordial crust. Hadean magma ocean solidified ~200 Myr. Zircons: first stable crust 4.4 BYA.

Water origin debate: ~50‑70% carbonaceous chondrites, 20‑30% mantle outgassing, <10% comets.

4. Internal Structure & Layers

← Inner Core (solid Fe-Ni) ← Outer Core (liquid) ← Lower Mantle ← Transition Zone ← Upper Mantle ← Crust ← Hydrosphere ← Atmosphere ← Magnetosphere

Inner core

Radius 1220 km, T 5200-6000 K, P 330-360 GPa, density 12.8-13.1 g/cm³, solid hcp iron, grows ~1mm/yr.

Outer core

Thickness 2260 km, liquid Fe+Ni+light elements, T 4000-5500 K, source of magnetic field.

Core-mantle boundary (D'')

Depth 2891 km, ultra-low velocity zones, LLSVPs beneath Africa & Pacific.

Lower mantle

660-2891 km, perovskite + magnesiowüstite, T 1900-4000 K.

Transition zone

410-660 km, wadsleyite, ringwoodite, potential water storage (several oceans).

Upper mantle & Crust

Peridotite, lithosphere + asthenosphere. Oceanic crust 5-10 km (basalt), continental 25-70 km (granite).

Hydrosphere

Oceans 96.5% (1.335×10⁹ km³), ice 1.76%, groundwater 1.69%. Total water 1.386×10⁹ km³. Mantle hidden water maybe 5-10× surface.

Atmosphere

N₂ 78.08%, O₂ 20.95%, Ar 0.93%, CO₂ 417ppm. Layers: troposphere, stratosphere, mesosphere, thermosphere, exosphere.

Magnetosphere: dynamo, teardrop shape, Van Allen belts.

5. Energy & Heat

Total heat flow 47±2 TW. Radiogenic 20-25 TW, primordial 12-20 TW, core cooling 5-15 TW, tidal 0.3 TW. Surface energy: 340 W/m² in, 239 W/m² out.

6. Temperature Profile

Surface avg 15°C, core center 5200-6000°C. Geothermal gradient ~25-30°C/km. Record extremes: 56.7°C (Death Valley) / -89.2°C (Vostok).

7. Pressure Profile

8. Density Profile

9. Rotation & Orbit

Orbital period 365.256 days, eccentricity 0.0167, velocity 29.78 km/s. Sidereal day 23h56m, solar day 24h. Axial tilt 23.439°, decreasing. Milankovitch cycles: eccentricity 100/413ky, obliquity 41ky, precession 19-23ky.

10. Position in Galaxy

26,660 ly from galactic center, Orion Arm, galactic orbit 230 km/s, period ~225-250 Myr. Nearest star Proxima Centauri 4.24 ly.

11. Plate Tectonics

Major plates: Pacific, North American, Eurasian, African, Antarctic, Indo-Australian, South American. Boundaries: divergent, convergent, transform. Slab pull 80-90% driving force. Wilson cycle supercontinent Pangea 335-175 Mya; next Pangea Ultima ~250 My from now.

12. Magnetic Field & Magnetosphere

Magnetic moment 7.79×10²² Am², surface field ~45 μT. Dynamo in outer core. Reversals every 0.1-5 per Myr, last 780,000 yrs. Aurora green (O) red (high O) blue/purple (N).

13. Weather & Climate

Hadley, Ferrel, Polar cells. Jet streams. Thermohaline circulation ~1000yr. Climate zones tropical to polar. Extreme weather: tropical cyclones (80-100/yr), tornadoes, lightning.

14. Soil & Regolith

Soil horizons O‑A‑E‑B‑C‑R. Soil types: Mollisols, Alfisols, Aridisols, Ultisols, Oxisols, Histosols, Andisols. Composition 45% mineral, 25% water, 25% air, 5% organic.

15. Life on Earth

Species described ~1.5-2M, estimated 8-100M. Biomass 550 Gt C: plants 82%, bacteria 13%, fungi 2%, animals 0.4% (humans 0.01%). First life 3.8-4.0 BYA. Extremophiles in vents, ice, deep subsurface, acid, Dead Sea.

16. Alien Life & SETI

Drake eq: optimistic N=60,000, pessimistic extremely low. Fermi paradox resolutions. Search methods: radio, optical, biosignatures. Known exoplanets 5500+, habitable zone ~100-200.

17. Light & Emissions

Sunlight peak 502nm. Airglow, aurora, bioluminescence, lava glow. Earth's spectrum visible to radio. 80% light pollution.

18. Gravity & Dynamics

19. Black Hole Possibility
